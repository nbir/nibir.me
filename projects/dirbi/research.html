objective

This project is aimed at understanding the influence of socio-cultural background in user agreement analysis. The primary objective will be a classification task to tell apart agreement and disagreement in people using multimodal analysis of audio-visual features. The experiments will be carried out independently on data pertaining to people of American, Chinese and Indian origin. We also wish to statistically analyze behavioral cues (/features) to find the ones that are independent of the socio-cultural background of a person.


dataset

We choose the domain of two person sit-down interviews, as such interviews are often conducted by television agencies and the videos are available on YouTube. In such videos the camera switches between the person being interviewed and the interviewer, and sometimes showing both of them. Since the primary focus is on the person being interviewed, we are interested only in those portions of the videos.

We wish to build a dataset comprising of 25 videos each of American, Chinese and Indian people in such interviews. As interviews usually span a long time, we would trim the videos to a minute length, and make sure they contain instances of agreement and disagreement. We will be limiting our study to interviews conducted in English language only. Although, interviews in native languages would be expressive (and more interesting to analyze), they would be difficult to annotate.


features & annotation

Bousmalis et al. (2009) provides a comprehensive list of cues (head gestures, facial expressions and body postures) that are predominant in agreement and disagreement. While cues like head nod, smile, eyebrow raise, laughter and agreement word are helpful in identifying agreement, head shake, eye roll, jaw drop, lip bite/pucker, arm folding, sighing, throat clearing, hand actions etc. determine disagreement.

We will be looking at features from multiple modes of communication â€“ vocal, head movement, facial expressions and hand gestures.

Annotation will be done for all such portions of the video where the person being interviewed expresses agreement or disagreement.


qualitative analysis / statistical analysis

Statistical analysis of individual features will be done to find features that are independent of the speaker's cultural background.

A simple example will be to consider smile as a feature, and three vectors as [smile in agreement in American data], [smile in agreement in Chinese data] and [smile in agreement in Indian data]. Statistical tests can be performed to check if they are independent.


machine learning objective

The machine learning objective will be to classify agreement and disagreement. We will train separate classifier models for each of the three socio-cultural backgrounds, and cross check them to find if a classifier trained using data pertaining to people from one socio-cultural background performs well when tested on data from another socio-cultural background.

Further, a classifier model will be trained using only those features that are independent of socio-cultural background and the accuracies will be checked to find any difference.


Reference:
K. Bousmalis, M. Mehu, and M. Pantic. Spotting agreement and disagreement: A survey of nonverbal audiovisual cues and tools. In Proc. IEEE Int'l Conf. Affective Computing and Intelligent Interaction, 2009, pp. 1-9
